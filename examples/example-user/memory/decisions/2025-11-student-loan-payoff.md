# Student loan — paid off ahead of schedule

**Date:** 2025-11-20.

**What happened:** paid off the Nelnet student loan in full. Remaining principal at payoff: $9,100 at 5.5% APR (original balance $28k from 2016). The scheduled payoff date on the standard 10-year amortization would've been 2026-08.

**How it was funded:**
- 2025 bonus — Alex redirected the "top-priority" 60% bucket (per `memory/preferences/windfall-split.md`) to the loan instead of the then-top-priority EF rebuild. Roughly $7,200 of the $12k after-tax bonus.
- Plus $1,900 from the HYSA — a deliberate drawdown, not an emergency tap.

**Why we chose this over keeping the EF rebuild as top priority:**
1. Loan APR (5.5%) was above HYSA yield (4.25%) — net negative carry of ~1.25% on every dollar held in HYSA that could've been loan principal.
2. Loan interest was no longer tax-deductible for Alex (income phase-out).
3. Emotional: Alex said "I've been paying this thing for 9 years. I want it off my ledger." That counts.

**What the math said:** holding the loan to 2026-08 would've cost ~$185 more in interest vs. this early payoff. Not huge. The bigger win was the monthly cashflow change — $220/mo freed.

**Downstream consequence:**
- That $220/mo is now redirected to the Chase Sapphire CC per `state/debts.md`, which is why the CC is on track to hit $0 by 2026-08 instead of late 2026.
- EF rebuild took a temporary hit; re-prioritized as top bucket in 2026 windfall splits until it crosses $15k.

**Status:** closed. Kept in memory for "why is EF recovery slower than expected in early 2026?" lookups.
