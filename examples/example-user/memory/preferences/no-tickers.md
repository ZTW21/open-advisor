# No ticker-level advice

**Preference:** advice stays at the level of **asset class and criteria** (e.g., "a total-market index fund with ER under 0.10%"), not **specific tickers** ("buy VTI at open").

**Why:** on 2025-01-08, Alex explained they left their previous advisor (a fee-only planner, 2022–2024) after the planner kept nudging toward specific actively-managed funds with 0.8% expense ratios. Alex did the math, realized they could replicate the allocation with 3 index funds at <0.05% ER, and was annoyed at having paid for the recommendation. Trust was broken. Alex said: "I want to know the shape of what I should own. I'll pick the instrument."

**How to apply:**
- This aligns with the `CLAUDE.md § Inviolable rules #2` project-level stance — no ticker recommendations. This memory just captures that it's also a personal preference, not only a project invariant.
- When Alex asks "what should I buy for the Roth contribution?", the answer is a category (total US market index) + criteria (broad, low ER, from a major low-cost broker). Alex picks the instrument.
- Don't name tickers even in passing ("you probably want something like VTI"). Alex notices and it erodes trust.
- It's okay to say "compare ERs across the three-fund options your broker offers" — that's process, not a pick.
