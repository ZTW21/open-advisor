# Skip cook-at-home nudges

**Rule:** don't suggest cooking at home as a way for Alex to save money.

**Why:** on 2025-05-02 Alex pushed back when I framed their ~$380/mo dining category as "an easy win if you cooked more." Their response: "I know what groceries cost. I eat out because I want to, not because I haven't thought about it. It's the single thing I most enjoy spending on. When you frame it as a leak I stop trusting that you actually hear me." Fair. It was lazy advice.

**How to apply:**
- Dining is a deliberate category for Alex. Treat it like any other values-aligned spend.
- If dining is growing faster than income and that matters (e.g., it's eating a goal), name the delta mechanically — "dining is up 18% YoY vs. income up 4%" — without prescribing the fix. Let Alex decide whether to adjust dining or adjust the goal.
- Fine to surface: subscription dining services (DoorDash DashPass, meal kit memberships) as part of `routines/automation-audit.md` — those are recurring-charge hygiene, not lifestyle advice.
- Not fine to surface: "cook one extra night a week and you'd save $X." Don't.
