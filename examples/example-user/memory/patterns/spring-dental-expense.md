# Biennial spring dental expense

**Pattern:** Every two years, Q1, a $3,000–$4,000 dental expense lands. Observed:
- 2022-02: crown work, $3,200.
- 2024-03: implant follow-up, $3,800.
- 2026-02: crown replacement, $3,400 (confirmed in `transactions/` via `finance cashflow --last 90d`).

**Source of the pattern:** older dental work from the 2010s is cycling through replacements on a ~10-year crown lifespan. Alex's dentist has flagged two more crowns as likely candidates in the next decade.

**Why this matters:**
- This is not an emergency. It's a predictable recurring expense that the emergency fund should not have to absorb.
- **Next expected hit:** Q1 2028 (~$3,500 in today's dollars; inflate modestly by then).
- Per `rules.md § Cash`: HYSA should be sized to include this as a named sinking fund by late 2027, not just general EF.

**How to apply:**
- In monthly reports during 2027, flag "biennial dental due early 2028 — ~$3,500 — is this carved out of HSA + HYSA, or does it need its own sinking fund?"
- Don't confuse it with an emergency if it lands on schedule. If it lands early (e.g., an unexpected root canal in 2027), that's a distinct event.
