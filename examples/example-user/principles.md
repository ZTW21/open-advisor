# Investing Principles

*Fictional example. Boglehead template with small overrides.*

## Core beliefs

- Time in the market beats timing the market. I will not try to time exits during drawdowns.
- Costs compound. Expense ratios over 0.10% need a strong justification; anything over 0.25% is off-limits for new money.
- Diversification is the only free lunch. Broad-market index funds over stock picks, always.
- Taxes are real. Tax-advantaged space (401k, Roth IRA, HSA) goes first.
- Emotions are the enemy. Written rules > gut feel during volatility.

## Target allocation

Long-horizon (retirement bucket, 25+ years):

- **Equities: 85%** — split 65% US total market, 20% international total market.
- **Bonds: 15%** — short + intermediate treasury bond fund.

Emergency fund and short-term savings (≤3 years) stay in HYSA or equivalent, not in this allocation.

## Rebalancing

- Drift threshold: rebalance when any asset class is >5 percentage points off target.
- Prefer rebalancing with new contributions over selling.
- Annual tax-loss harvest review in December.

## When I deviate from this

- Keeping some older tech-heavy single-stock positions from a prior job. Not selling while the unrealized gain would push me into a higher bracket. Will revisit at retirement or if a single position crosses the 5% rule.
- 6% of net worth in BTC. Above the 5% ceiling in `rules.md` and I know it. Plan is to let it drift back under 5% rather than rebalance down, per the note in `memory/decisions/2025-09-btc-position.md`.

## Override notes

If a future advisor (or future me) wants to change any of the above, write the reasoning in `memory/decisions/` first. Rule drift without a paper trail is how people lose money.
