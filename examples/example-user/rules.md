# Rules

*Fictional example. Written by the user during onboarding; reviewed annually.*

## Cash & liquidity
- Keep at least $5,000 liquid (checking + HYSA combined) at all times. Emergency-fund rebuild is a separate target; this is the floor.

## Debt
- No new credit card debt. The current $2,800 balance is the last one; paying it down is non-negotiable.
- No borrowing to invest. Ever.

## Investing
- No single stock over 5% of invested assets.
- No options, leverage, crypto over 5% of net worth, or meme trades.
- Index funds only for new contributions. Existing holdings that aren't index funds can stay if selling them would realize meaningful tax.

## Spending
- No subscriptions added without a 30-day cooling-off window. If I still want it after 30 days, fine.
- Annual "unused subscription" sweep in April and October.

## Windfalls
- Default split for bonuses/refunds/gifts over $500: 60% to the nearest active goal, 30% to Roth IRA, 10% to discretionary. Override requires writing a reason.

## Lifestyle
- No withdrawing from retirement accounts before 59½ unless a real emergency. A "good opportunity" is not an emergency.
